04 — Acquisition Process

Acquisition Process

Transfer is not transactional. It follows a defined protocol designed to protect the integrity of the asset and the interests of both parties.

—
No public auction or open bidding
—
No retail domain marketplace listing
—
No low-context or speculative resale
—
No transfer without verified identity and escrow

Transfer proceeds according to the following protocol:

01
Qualified inquiry submitted via the contact channel below, with context about the acquirer’s identity and intended use.
02
Initial review of acquirer profile against the qualified buyer criteria.
03
Confidential discussion of acquisition terms, asset scope, and transfer conditions.
04
Agreed terms documented and verified identity confirmed on both sides.
05
Secure escrow arrangement established through a recognised domain transfer intermediary.
06
Domain and associated assets transferred in full upon escrow confirmation.
05 — Why Qualification Is Required

Why Qualification Is Required

GigaSaudi.com is not a commodity. It is a category name.

A category name transferred to the wrong acquirer is a category name destroyed. A category name in the hands of the right acquirer becomes infrastructure.

The qualification requirement exists to protect the strategic integrity of the asset — for the acquirer as much as for the category itself.

A name that names Saudi Arabia’s giga-scale era at the highest level of abstraction carries a weight that most domain transactions do not. The qualification process is how that weight is acknowledged at the point of transfer.
