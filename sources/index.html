01    Source Policy

How Claims Are Made

GigaSaudi.com makes three categories of claims. Each type is governed by a different evidentiary standard.

Type A
Verified
Directly supported by official government communications, sovereign fund disclosures, international institutional announcements, or credible financial reporting. Stated as fact without qualification. Example: “Saudi Arabia will host the FIFA World Cup 2034.” — FIFA official announcement, October 2024.
Type B
Qualified
Well-supported by public reporting but expressed in directional or range terms rather than precise figures. Stated with appropriate hedging language. Example: “PIF is one of the world’s largest and most strategically visible sovereign wealth funds, with publicly stated targets in the trillions of dollars.”
Type C
Architectural
Analytical framing and compound reasoning that does not make specific numerical claims. Editorial judgment about observable, well-reported phenomena. Example: “Saudi Arabia is increasingly understood through scale rather than geography alone.”

Language Standards

Preferred
  • “Saudi Arabia is undergoing a giga-scale transformation across capital, cities, tourism, technology, energy, sport, culture, and global perception.”
  • “PIF is one of the world’s largest and most strategically visible sovereign wealth funds.”
  • “Trillions in publicly stated capital commitments.”
  • “Vision 2030 targets include…”
  • “Accelerating investment across sectors.”
Avoided
  • “Saudi Arabia is the largest / richest / most advanced / guaranteed to succeed.”
  • “PIF is the world’s largest sovereign wealth fund.”
  • “$X trillion guaranteed.”
  • “Will achieve by 2030.”
  • Precise financial figures without verified attribution.

Any precise financial figure, search-volume estimate, project cost, or timeline appearing in GigaSaudi.com content either cites a specific source or is accompanied by a qualification that it is approximate, directional, or subject to revision.
