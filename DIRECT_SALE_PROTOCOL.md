## Asset

GigaSaudi.com is an existing strategic digital asset. It is not a raw domain listing. The asset encompasses the domain name, the current public web surface, the strategic positioning framework, the category-native framing and reference materials, and the repository materials developed around Saudi Arabia’s giga-scale transformation.

Do not respond to acquisition inquiries that arrive through any other channel without first verifying they have been routed through or acknowledged by Sohadot.

## Qualified Inquiry Requirement

An inquiry is qualified when it includes, without prompting, all of the following:

1. Acquirer organisation name and description
2. Intended use of the asset
3. Acquisition context (why now, why this asset)
4. Contact identity sufficient to verify the organisation

Inquiries that consist only of a price request, a broker introduction without a named buyer, or a one-line expression of interest are not qualified and do not require substantive response.

## Transaction Scope

GigaSaudi.com is offered as an existing strategic digital asset. Transaction scope is not a tiered menu of options — it is agreed in writing between the parties and documented before any transfer is initiated.

The asset being transferred encompasses the domain name and the transferable materials associated with the existing strategic digital asset as built. Transferable components from that asset are defined in the written transaction terms according to what the qualified acquirer requires and what the parties agree in writing.

Typical transferable materials include:
- The domain name and DNS control
- The current public website repository and all associated files
- Internal acquisition-support documents prepared for buyer review

Additional scope (structured handover documentation, transition coordination, or supplementary materials) may be incorporated into the transaction terms when applicable and agreed in writing.

**No post-sale development, operation, consulting, advisory work, or future content production is included in any transaction unless explicitly and separately agreed in writing and added to the transaction terms.**

## Escrow Requirement

No transfer proceeds without a recognised escrow arrangement in place and buyer funds confirmed as secured.

Accepted escrow providers include established domain transaction escrow services recognised by the domain industry. The specific provider is agreed between parties before the transaction opens.

Escrow fees: responsibility to be agreed in writing as part of transaction terms. Standard arrangement is buyer-pays or split equally; not seller-pays.

---

## Transfer Method

**Registrar Push (preferred where registrar eligibility allows):**  
Domain is pushed within the same registrar to a buyer account at the same registrar. Fastest method. No auth code required. Buyer must have an account at the current registrar.

**Registrar Transfer (used when buyer prefers a different registrar):**  
Standard EPP auth code transfer. Domain unlock required. 60-day transfer lock applies after some registrar-level changes — confirm timing before initiating. ICANN transfer process applies. Transfer typically takes 5–7 days.

Transfer method agreed with buyer before escrow opens.

## Buyer Verification

Before transfer is initiated, buyer identity is confirmed by:

1. Organisation name matches the inquiry context
2. Contact email on record matches an address associated with the stated organisation
3. Escrow account is opened under a verified name matching the buyer’s stated identity

Anonymous buyers or buyers whose identity cannot be verified do not proceed to transfer.

---

## Seller Verification

Buyer may verify seller identity through:

1. Current WHOIS registrant information (where not privacy-protected) or registrar-confirmed ownership
2. Domain control verification: seller demonstrates control by placing a verification string in DNS or at a specified URL path on gigasaudi.com upon request
3. Escrow account opened under seller identity matching the above

---

## Payment Custody

1. Buyer deposits full agreed purchase price into escrow before any transfer action is taken
2. Escrow service confirms receipt and holds funds in custody
3. Seller is notified of escrow confirmation before initiating transfer
4. No partial payments, staged payments, or payments outside escrow are accepted

---

## Buyer Confirmation

After domain transfer completes:

1. Buyer confirms in writing (email to escrow and to agent@sohadot.com) that the domain has been received and is under buyer’s control
2. Confirmation includes: domain name, buyer registrar account, date of confirmed control
3. Escrow holds funds until confirmation is received
4. If buyer does not confirm within the inspection period, escrow follows its standard dispute resolution process

---

## Seller Payout

Funds are released from escrow to seller only after:

1. Buyer confirmation of successful receipt is received
2. Inspection period (if applicable) has elapsed without dispute
3. Escrow service processes the release per its standard terms

---

## Excluded Items (Unless Separately Agreed)

The following are not included in any transaction unless explicitly negotiated and documented in the transaction terms:

- Post-sale development, operation, or content production of any kind
- Ongoing consulting, advisory, or editorial services from the seller
- Rights to claim any official Saudi government affiliation (which does not exist)
- Access to private correspondence, inquiry records, or non-public communications
- Any revenue guarantee, traffic guarantee, or performance warranty
- Email accounts or email history associated with gigasaudi.com
- Exclusivity over the terms “Giga” or “Saudi” individually in other contexts
- Social media handles or accounts (if any exist)

## Closing Sequence

1. Qualified inquiry received and reviewed
2. Buyer profile assessed against qualified buyer criteria
3. Confidential discussion of scope, terms, and conditions
4. Transaction scope agreed in writing (see ACQUISITION_TERMS_DRAFT.md)
5. Escrow provider agreed and escrow transaction opened
6. Buyer funds escrow
7. Escrow confirms secured funds — seller notified
8. Domain unlock and auth code obtained (if registrar transfer) or push prepared (if same-registrar)
9. Transfer initiated
10. Buyer confirms receipt
11. Escrow releases funds to seller
12. Transaction records archived
13. Website and repository status updated post-closing as agreed
